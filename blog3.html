Organic vs Paid Social Media: Finding a Happy Medium

Paid advertising can open the floodgates for brand awareness and lead generation, but it doesn’t mean that it should be reserved as your only strategy.

According to Hootsuite, the average organic reach for a Facebook post is 5.17% of a Page’s likes, while the average paid reach is 28.1% of the total likes, a difference of almost 23%!
Paid advertising can open the floodgates for brand awareness and lead generation, but it doesn’t mean that it should be reserved as your only strategy.
Let’s take a look at how we can make an organic strategy work for you and how you can blend it with paid social to get the most out of your time and budget.

Why is Organic Social Media Important?

You can grow your brand’s online presence considerably by using social media platforms and make use of the insights and analytics they natively provide, there’s no better way to build your brand online and nurture relationships with your following.
Let’s look at what else Organic Social can do for you.

  • It gives your audience an outlet to connect with you.
  • Help you listen to the opinions of your following and improve your brand.
  • Form a sense of trust and demonstrate your transparency to the world.
  • It gives you a platform to tell your story
  • It won’t hurt your budget, it’s free!

Social media can also help generate user-generated content through free hashtag campaigns, taking a load off of your marketing teams & get your brand name buzzing.
Dove has always been a promoter of inclusivity and body positivity with their campaigns, their #ShowUs campaign aimed to challenge beauty stereotypes, Dove chose to partner with women around the globe after discovering that over 70% of women didn’t feel represented in advertising and media.

The campaign was an absolute hit on the internet, garnering them over 650,000 mentions via the hashtag and over 5000 images in their photo library. There’s no better proof of how a social media campaign can help you connect and relate to your audience.
Sometimes you need a little boost to back up the hard work you put into organic growth, and that’s where paid social comes in.

What Can Paid Social Do For You?

To be honest with you, this is just a fancy term for advertising. Paid Social is when brands pay platforms like Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, etc to reach audiences that would have never discovered their existence otherwise while helping them target specific segments.
Let’s see what else Paid Social can do for you.

  • Reinforce the messages and values you portray through your organic efforts
  • Help boost current conversion levels, especially if you have a marketing strategy that converts well.
  • Overcome the limitations of ranking algorithms on most platforms

The American marketing chain Publix consistently uses geo-targeting (advertising based on consumer location & behaviour ) to promote its ads to consumers aged 21 and above near their store locations.

If your brand has a product/ service being advertised that has a favourable number of conversions, leveraging the power of a paid campaign will promote your offerings to a wider audience and bump up your conversions significantly.
Now we know that everything in life has to be balanced, and it may leave you wondering.

How Can I Get The Best of Both Worlds?

Although organic reach may not bring you wild numbers in terms of conversions and leads, the benefits it brings are still significant.

Your brand should focus on adding value, taking care not to sound too sales-oriented in each post and work toward extending your network organically. Experiment with organic reach before making the move to a hybrid strategy.
The meditation app Headspace successfully uses a combination of paid and organic social to generate brand awareness.
Their organic strategy focuses on fostering a community by providing useful tips and insights that are appreciated by their following, whereas their paid campaigns reflect on these values while using engaging visuals that address customer pain points, depicting how users feel before and after using the app.

Conclusion

Paid and organic social media can be wildly different animals, to create a complete marketing strategy a brand needs to find a middle ground between them to have an edge on social media.
Engage your viewers and give them a sense of belonging and foster a community around your brand, this can then be strengthened with the power of paid campaigns, craft a symphony of paid vs organic and combine them to bring out the most from your campaigns.
