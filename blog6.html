TikTok: Marketing Trend or Mainstay?

Coming in as one of the most used social network with 689 million monthly users as of January 2021, TikTok seems to be here to stay.

TikTok is built on the concept of 15-second short-form videos where users can incorporate filters, music and a host of video effects, and unsurprisingly TikTok has become famous for users lip-syncing and dancing to songs and has been the launchpad for many viral challenges.
But creators on TikTok are also using the platform for more far-reaching topics such as financial tips, healthcare and fitness.
Wondering how it all works? Allow us to explain.

The Nitty-Gritty of TikTok

TikTok demonstrates its mastery of psychology and User Experience with an immersive, fullscreen experience that is totally distraction-free.
TikTok focuses on creating curiosity and giving its users instant gratification. It caters to the short attention span of the younger generation and supplies emotionally charging content to draw them in.
It also relies on behaviour based personalization where it understands what a user wants and pulls you back into a rabbit hole of engaging content. They mix the simple task of swiping with the highly addictive variety of content in order to create a habit-forming system that gets you to swipe up and keep going endlessly.
TikTok seems to do a stellar job of retaining user attention, as the average user tends to spend around 45 minutes a day browsing the platform.
But how do content creators retain their following?

The Tactics of TikTok

Successful content creators identify how to grow their presence and nurture their audience. Some take the easy way out by using paid services to grow their following, which never results in long term success, here are a few things done by creators who use the platform to its fullest, your brand can also leverage these tactics to organically grow a large following.

  • Identify your target audience
  • Follow and take advantage of trends & challenges
  • Create content that educates & resonates with your followers
  • Identify the right times to post by using your analytics
  • Engage with other brands & creators

Since you’re still reading you might be wondering how incorporating TikTok into your marketing strategy would affect or transform your business, without further ado let’s dive into the pros and cons of TikTok as a marketing tool.

Pros

With hundreds of millions of daily users, TikTok is a solid platform to gain tons of exposure. It’s an ideal place for your business to explore creative ways to promote a product, increase awareness and grow an organic following.
TikTok is a good bet if you intend on showing behind the scenes content, it allows your brand to show its playful and humane side. Local brands like Daraz.lk use the platform for funny bloopers and BTS content which give their followers a sneak peek of the people behind their brands and their personalities.
You also can verify yourself and add authenticity to your profile. TikTok also gives you in-depth analytics at your fingertips which makes this an excellent tool for a marketers Journal
It’s also prime real estate for virality, keep a close eye on the trends and challenges that take the platform by storm and take part in the ones that you feel will put you on the map.

Cons

Even though the platform has good analytics, they only last for 28 days. If you plan on tracking your engagement and reach in the long term, you may have to come up with your method of maintaining the information, a spreadsheet perhaps?.
TikTok currently has no way for you to easily link a video back to your website, on the flipside this presents an opportunity for your brand to get creative.
The nature of TikTok means that brands have to constantly keep coming up with new content or keeping track of current trends and challenges, cutting through the noise can be a considerable challenge.
TikTok & their parent company ByteDance have come under fire for privacy concerns regarding data sharing with China. For the most part, this has now been resolved and is said to pose only as much risk as any other social media application.
Now that we’ve taken you through the pros and cons, let’s look at how you can market your brand on TikTok.

How Can You Market Yourself on TikTok

TikTok offers exceptional marketing opportunities, but being that you are advertising to a predominantly younger audience you must get your message across immediately.
TikTok’s own analytics say that 63% of all videos with the highest click-through rate (CTR) on their platform get their key message through within the first 3 seconds.
Here are 3 key ways for you to market your brand on the platform.

1. Create a hashtag challenge

This is a great way to start a trend of your own, a well-performing challenge can attract millions of user-generated videos, which are in other words, free advertising for your brand. TikTok estimates that over 35% of its user base take part in trending hashtag challenges.

The #ReadySETgo challenge by Laura Mercier is a good example of a successful hashtag challenge, they teamed up with 5 beauty influencers who had a combined reach of over 19 million followers. They created a before & after transition video showcasing the product’s results which resulted in over 8.1 billion impressions and 2500 pieces of UGC.

2. Collaborate with influencers

As mentioned above, a brand can take its plans to the next level if they team up with credible influencers who have a large following.
Influencers who eat, sleep and breathe TikTok can save you valuable time and money as they already know how to leverage the platforms trends to their advantage. TikTok has realized this and came up with ‘TikTok Marketplace’, a native platform to connect brands & influencers.
Dunkin saw great success after teaming up with TikTok’s most-followed star, Charli D’Amelio. The influencer is seen drinking their coffee in many of her videos, Dunkin has reported a 45% increase in sales following the partnership.

3. Leverage TikTok ads

TikTok is the new hotspot for advertising, it has as much reach as Instagram but isn’t drowning with advertisements. This makes it ripe for cost-effective brand impressions.
When it comes to advertising, TikTok offers 5 different approaches

  • Branded Hastags
    • Sponsored hashtag challenges that aim to help advertisers generate User-Generated Content (UGC) and achieve greater levels of engagement (averaging 8.5%)
  • Brand Takeovers
    • This type of ad takes over the Home feed upon opening the app, these run around 3-5 seconds in length, these can lead users to a hashtag challenge or landing page, these are on the costlier end of TikTok adverts.
  • Branded Effects
    • Similar to Snapchat filters, advertisers can create special effects, stickers and filters to promote themselves and are one of the more popular methods of advertising on the platform.
  • Top View Ads
    • Pop up just as the app is fired up, around 60 seconds in length and more effective than in-feed ads but cost more.
  • In-feed Ads
    • Show up in the Home feed, usually 15 seconds long, best used by small businesses due to their cost

Still not convinced? Here are a few big brands that reaped the benefits of TikTok advertising

  • Puma
    • The athletic corporation decided to go down the Branded Effect route and combined it with the #FlashOfFuture hashtag, the campaign was wildly successful netting them over 587.5 million views.
  • E.L.F Cosmetics
    • The American cosmetics brand E.L.F Cosmetics went to the extent of creating a custom soundtrack for their #eyeslipsface campaign, which on its own went on to become a hit song with well over 2.5 million views on YouTube and becoming #4 on Spotify’s Global Viral charts
      Their campaign ended up becoming the most viral on the platform to date, nearly 5 million users participated and the UGC they created gathered stands at 7.7 billion views.

Conclusion

Now that we’ve explored TikTok in detail, we can gather that there’s more than meets the eye, and it definitely takes some time to get used to its fast-paced operation.
As with many things, given some time you can begin to see the benefits, it’s a very cost-effective way of mass marketing and can even help you overtake your competitors by leaps and bounds. If they haven’t taken the time to learn about TikTok, this could be an ace up your sleeve.
